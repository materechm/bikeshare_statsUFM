Title: Microglia Induce Motor Neuron Death via the Classical NF-κB Pathway in Amyotrophic Lateral Sclerosis

Year Published: 2014

Observation: NF-kB inhibition in astrocytes doesn't contribute to neuroprotection in vivo or in vitro. Microglia contribute to NF-kB activation during ALS progresion. SOD1-G93A microglia induce MN death in an NF-kB dependent manner. NF-kB activation in enough to induce MN death in vitro

Comment: Localization of NF-kB activity and deletion of NF-kB signaling in microglia rescued MNs from microglial mediated death in vitro and extended survival of SOD1 mice

Citation: Frakes, A. E., Ferraiuolo, L., Haidet-Phillips, A. M., Schmelzer, L., Braun, L., Miranda, C. J., … Kaspar, B. K. (2014). Microglia Induce Motor Neuron Death via the Classical NF-κB Pathway in Amyotrophic Lateral Sclerosis. Neuron, 81(5), 1009–23. doi:10.1016/j.neuron.2014.01.013
